Carbon capture, waste valorization, renewables, and carbon markets exist today as isolated silos — each solving a fragment. Carbon Chakra is the connective architecture that lets them operate as one system.

Small and mid-sized industries plug in through the Carbon Milk Run — a shared collection network that aggregates emissions into regional processing hubs. Inside each hub, capture, liquefaction, catalysis, and AI dispatch route every stream to its highest-value destination: specialty PCC, carbon-negative pellets, hydroponic feedstock, methanol, SAF, or certified credits.

Pillar Two — The Integrated System

This is how
every industry connects.

The diagram below is the complete Carbon Chakra operating system. On the sides, small and mid-sized industries plug in through micro-capture units at each source. Milk-run trucks aggregate their CO₂ into the central Carbon Hub, which is powered by solar and wind, and runs three processing bays — capture, liquefaction, extrusion. The hub's outputs fan out below as five revenue streams. A circular loop closes the system: credit revenue funds the next cycle of capture.

◈ Spoke · Micro-Capture
Modular capture units at foundries, kilns, ceramics, textiles, cement, and food plants. Each stores CO₂ on-site in cylinders.
→ Logistics · Milk Run
Smart routing collects filled cylinders on scheduled loops — the same shared truck serves every industry in the ring.
◉ Hub · Processing
Central facility runs capture → liquefaction → press & extruder bays, powered by on-site solar and wind, with a CO₂ buffer tank.
↓ Outputs · Revenue
Five parallel streams: specialty PCC, carbon-negative pellets, hydroponic feedstock, BEE-certified credits, methanol/SAF/H₂.
